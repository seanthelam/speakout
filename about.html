In 2023, Colorado College students Owen Blazick, Sean Lam, and Jimmy O'Connell decided to take Racial Inequality - a class focused on the study of race as a dimension of inequality. They learned about individual and institutional forms of racism and discrimination, looking at historical, comparative, and theoretical perspectives. With this, they sought to better understand and see the impacts of racial inequality outside of the classroom. Owen, Sean, and Jimmy started conducting interviews to see how others viewed and experienced racial inequality. It was clear that this was something crucial to discuss about in the modern day. Thus, Speak Out was created as a space for other individuals to tell their own stories and read about others. It was also established for others to create proposals and demands, and to address and educate about racial inequality.

The mission of this website is to provide an anonymous space for students to not only tell their own experiences with racism and racial inequality in education, but also for students to educate themselves by reading othersâ€™ stories. We hope that this website contributes to a growing critical consciousness that addresses racial inequality not only at CC, but the higher education system at large.
